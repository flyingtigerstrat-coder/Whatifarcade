Four days on the harbor

The tall ships of the world gather in Boston for America's 250th. We won't just walk their decks — we'll be out on the water three times, aboard the Bloodhound at Charlestown, the Lynx off Wharf 8, and the schooner Adventure from Fan Pier.

The base

Home for the trip is the Hampton Inn in the Seaport — a short walk from where most of the fleet ties up, and one free Silver Line ride from the airport. Nearly everything below is on foot. The one harbor crossing is a ferry to Charlestown, and it doubles as the approach to our first sail. Three times, we leave the pier entirely.

Pier 1, first stop, no detours: the Coast Guard barque Eagle, America's Tall Ship, boards only until 4:00 today and then departs for good. USS Constitution — "Old Ironsides" — lies right behind her. This hour is the reason we came early.

Fish Pier: the Five Sisters

Germany's Gorch Fock and Portugal's Sagres both board until 8:00 tonight — no rush, no lines like midday. India's Sudarshini is alongside till 10.

Evening

The giants, lit up

Commonwealth Pier stays open late: Chile's Esmeralda boards till 10 and Peru's BAP Unión — the largest ship in the whole fleet — till 9. The two biggest hulls in Boston, floodlit, after the crowds thin. Dinner after, no clocks.

Good to know

The small stuff that makes it smooth

Our three sails

Bloodhound — Mon 4:00, Charlestown Navy Yard Pier 8. Lynx — Tue 10:00, Seaport Wharf 8. Adventure — Wed 9:00, Courthouse Docks, 2 Northern Ave. Aboard 30 minutes early, every time.

Dress for the water

Closed-toe shoes are required to board anything. Bring a windbreaker or fleece for the sails — it runs 10–15° cooler underway than on the pier. Hat, sunscreen, water.

Tuesday is the tight one

The Eagle and Gloria board only till 4:00, then sail for good. Off the Lynx, Uber straight to the Navy Yard (pin: Gate 1) — decks over docks until the clock clears at 4. Then the day is ours.

Boarding hours — the fine print

Docks run 10 a.m.–10 p.m., but every ship boards at her captain's discretion and many close early or depart mid-festival. Check sailboston.com/boarding-hours each morning — it decides the day's order.

One tap, everywhere

A weekly CharlieCard pass covers the Silver Line from the airport and the Charlestown ferry — the whole transit footprint on a single card.

The base

Hotel's on points, so the trip is essentially flights, food, and a $7.50 ferry. Walkable to nearly everything; the Seaport does the heavy lifting.
